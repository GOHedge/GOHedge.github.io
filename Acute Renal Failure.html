Overview

Acute impairment of renal function

Causes

1. Compromised renal perfusion e.g. dehydration, heart failure, shock (acute)
2. Obstructed urinary flow
3. Damage to renal tissue by infectious and inflammatory dieases (e.g.
glomerulonephritis), intoxications, nephrotoxic drugs.

Clinical features

1. Oliguria (urine flow <1 ml/kg/hour)
2. Generalised oedema
3. Hypertension, heart failure, dyspnoea
4. Nausea and vomiting, anorexia
5. Lethargy, convulsions

Differential diagnosis

1. Other renal disorders
2. Biventricular heart failure

Investigations

1. Urine analysis: for blood, proteins, leucocytes, casts
2. Urea, creatinine and electrolytes.
