Glomerulonephritis

Acute inflammation of the renal glomeruli (small blood  vessels in the kidney)

Cause

Immune reactions often following an infection - usually  1-5 weeks after a streptococcal skin or throat infection

Clinical features 

1. Common in children >3 years and adolescents
2. Haematuria (red, or tea-coloured urine)
3. Oedema: Puffiness of the face/around the eyes, less  commonly generalised body swelling 
4. Discomfort in the kidney area (abdominal or back pain)
5. Anorexia
6. General weakness (malaise)
7. High blood pressure for age, commonly presenting as  headaches, visual disturbances, vomiting, and occasionally pulmonary oedema with dyspnoea 
8. Convulsions (in hypertensive crisis)
9. Oliguria (passing little urine) as renal failure sets in
10. Evidence of primary streptococcal infection:
11. Usually as acute tonsillitis with cervical adenitis
12. Less often as skin sepsis.

Differential diagnosis 

1. Kidney infections e.g. TB, pyelonephritis 
2. Kidney tumours
3. Heart failure
4. Malnutrition
5. Allergic reactions

Investigations 

1. Urine: Protein, microscopy for RBCs and casts, WBCs
2. Blood: Urea (uraemia) and creatinine levels, ASOT,  electrolytes 
3. Ultrasound: Kidneys.

Management

Inflammatory kidney disease with oedema, hypertension  and oliguria should be referred to regional hospital for specialised management.

Treatment

1. Monitor urine output, BP, daily weight 
2. Restrict fluid input (in oliguria)
3. Restrict salt and regulate protein in the diet (in  oliguria) 
4. Avoid or use with caution any drugs excreted by the kidney (see section 7.1.3) 
5. Treat any continuing hypertension (see section 4.1.6) 
5. If post-streptococcal 
6. Treat primary streptococcal infection (10-day  course): phenoxymethylpenicillin 500 mg every  6 hours  Child: 10-20 mg/kg per dose 
7. Or Amoxicillin 500 mg every 8 hours for 10 days  Child: 20 mg/kg per dose  If allergic to penicillin 
8. Erythromycin 500 mg every 6 hours for 10 days  Child: 15 mg/kg per dose  For fluid overload (oedema) 
9. Furosemide 80 mg IV (slow bolus)  Child:1 mg/kg every 8-12 hours.
