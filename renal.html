Urethral Discharge Syndrome (Male)

Overview | Symptoms | Signs | Causes | Risk factors | Prevention | Diagnosis | Treatment | Questions & Answers | Gallery | Video Tutorials | Download Pdf & Text Books.

Overview

It refers to urethral discharge in men with or without dysuria, caused by a number of diseases usually spread by sexual intercourse, which produce similar manifestations in males and may be difficult to distinguish clinically  

Causes

1. Common: Neisseria gonorrhoea (causing gonorrhoea), Chlamydia trachomatis and Ureaplasma urealyticum 
2. Uncommon: Trichomonas vaginalis

Clinical features

1. Mucus or pus at the tip of the penis; staining underwear
2. Burning pain on passing urine (dysuria), frequent urination

Investigations

1. Pus swab: Gram stain, culture and sensitivity
2. Blood: Screen for syphilis and HIV
3. Examine patient carefully to confirm discharge

Treatment

1. Take history and examine the client. Milk urethra if discharge is not obvious 
2. Retract prepuce and examine for ulcers
3. Treat both patient and sexual partners
4. Advise abstinence or condom use medicines 
5. Ceftriaxone 250 mg IM or Cefixime 400 mg single dose plus
